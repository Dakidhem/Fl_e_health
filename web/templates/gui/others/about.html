About Us

We are students in our final year at the Higher School of Computer Science in Sidi Bel Abbes, Algeria. Our final study project focuses on developing a specialized website for federated learning in medical health and X-ray images.

Our goal is to leverage the power of federated learning to enable hospitals and doctors to collaborate in diagnosing medical conditions using a global model while ensuring patient data privacy and security.

Our team consists of:

  • Mehdi Kadiri
  • Hamza Kaddouri

Specifications of our website:

  • Specialization: Federated learning in medical health and X-ray images.
  • Target Users: Hospitals and doctors who wish to collaborate and improve diagnostic accuracy.
  • Data Management: Each hospital/doctor can manage their patient data securely on the platform.
  • Global Model: The website utilizes federated learning techniques to aggregate local models and create a shared global model.
  • Privacy Preservation: Patient data privacy is maintained through encryption, anonymization, and secure data handling.
  • Diagnostic Capabilities: Hospitals and doctors can use the global model for diagnostic purposes, benefiting from collective knowledge.

How it works:

  1. Hospitals and doctors create accounts and securely upload patient data, including X-ray images and related information.
  2. The website uses federated learning techniques to train a global model without directly accessing or sharing individual patient data.
  3. The global model is periodically updated by aggregating the knowledge learned from different hospitals and doctors.
  4. Using the global model, hospitals and doctors can make diagnostic predictions based on the collective knowledge of the participating institutions.
  5. Data privacy is maintained throughout the process, ensuring patient confidentiality and complying with privacy regulations.

Located in Sidi Bel Abbes, Algeria, we are excited to bring our knowledge and skills to create a valuable tool for the medical community. We strive to make a positive impact and contribute to the field of healthcare through our final study project.
